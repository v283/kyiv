We form the Passive with the verb to be and the past participle of the verb.

Usage of Present Simple:

  • We use the Passive when the action or the object is more important than the person or thing which does the action:
    English is spoken all over the world.

Using by:

We can use by to say who or what is responsible for the action:

  • The painting was bought by a very rich American.
  • Penicillin was invented by Alexander Fleming.

Wh-questions:

  • How is pasta made?
  • When was America discovered?
  • Why has the music been turned off?
