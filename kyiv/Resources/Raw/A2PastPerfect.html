The Past Perfect is formed with had and the past participle.

Usage of Present Simple:

We use Past Perfect to talk about what happened before another event in the past. It is used with the Past Simple.

At the airport I realised that I had left my passport at home.
(I left my passport at home before I went to the airport.)

Be careful with this common mistake!

The contraction ‘d can be had or would. Remember that we use an infinitive form after would and a past participle after had.

  • I‘d love to go to your party. (= would)
  • I noticed that he‘d eaten my cake. (= had)

Keep in mind

  • When we got to the station, the train left.
    (=We were at the station and then the train left.)
  • When we got to the station, the train had left.
    (=We arrived at the station after the train had left.)
